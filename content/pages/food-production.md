
# Food Production in Space

Food production in space represents one of the most critical challenges for long-duration missions. As the weight of the ship is extremely important to reduce the quantity of fuel needed, it is not possible to bring two years worth of food for the entire crew in the ship. Innovative solutions have to be found to produce food on board of the ship or directly on Mars.

## Microgravity Effects

When interviewing Lucie POULET, she explained to me the study of exchanges going on inside the plant when doing photosynthesis. For example, the plant consummates the energy of the sun and CO2 to produce oxygen and heat. When placed in microgravity, the production of heat is not evacuated in the air like it would on Earth, which let the heat "stuck" around the plant. This permanent heat around the plant slows down its growth. To solve this problem, we can simulate air mouvement by turning on a ventilation system. The challenge is to use the least energy possible to ventilate but juste enough to let the plant grow normally.

## Plant Types for Space

In the movie The Martian, you might remember Mark Watney growing potatoes from a bag of Thanksgiving dinner. Well potatoes are actually not the easiest vegetable to grow. During my exchange with Gioia MASSA, I discovered the "Pick and Eat" vegetables. Tomatoes or salad for example are easier to harvest by being above-ground vegetable. It is easy to control their growth and check whenever thay are fully developped and ready to be eaten.
